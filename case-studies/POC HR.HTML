ML-Driven Employee Turnover Prediction Concept

A proof-of-concept approach to predicting flight risk

Industry

Human Resources

Project Type

Proof of Concept

Technologies

Machine Learning, HR Analytics

Job Alignment Highlight

This work sample exemplifies my ability to leverage advanced AI and automation techniques to drive business process improvements in employee turnover prediction. In this project, I developed a machine learning solution that predicts employee turnover in BPO environments by integrating diverse HR data, applying algorithms such as Random Forest and XGBoost, and using SHAP analysis for model interpretability.

Introduction

This machine learning solution addresses high employee turnover in BPO environments through predictive analytics. By analyzing HR data patterns, it identifies employees at risk of leaving before they resign, enabling proactive intervention and informed retention strategies.

Three-Stage Process

  1. Data Collection: Integrates HR records, engagement surveys, team metrics, compensation data, and external market information.
  2. ML Processing: Uses Random Forest and XGBoost algorithms with SHAP analysis to recognize patterns, score risk, and pinpoint key factors influencing turnover.
  3. Actionable Insights: Delivers targeted retention strategies, focuses interventions, and tracks ROI through executive dashboards.

BPO Implementation Benefits

The Business Problem

Problem: High agent turnover increases training costs and disrupts service quality.

Solution: Proactive identification and intervention before agents consider leaving.

Result: Stabilized workforce, reduced costs, improved customer satisfaction.
